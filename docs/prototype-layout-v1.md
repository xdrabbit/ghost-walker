# Prototype Layout v1

## Objective

Build the first real, testable Ghost Walker prototype focused on one question:

> Can compliant cable assist make leg lift and forward swing easier and more natural?

---

## Rev 1 System Layout

### Anchors

**Pelvic Anchor**
- reinforced belt (lifting belt works well)
- positioned low and tight
- must resist upward pull and twisting

**Thigh Anchor**
- high on femur
- padded cuff
- adjustable angle mounting point

---

## Force Path

### Recommended v1: lateral/anterior pull

- actuator mounted on rear-lateral waist
- cable runs forward along side of pelvis
- cable terminates on upper thigh cuff (slightly forward of hip line)

Goal: produce forward + upward assist (hip flex moment)

---

## Compliance

### Series compliance (recommended)

Insert elastic element between actuator and cable output:

Options:
- latex tubing
- elastomer strap
- extension spring (soft)

Purpose:
- smooth force ramp
- prevent jerk
- allow follow-through

---

## Actuator (v1 practical)

- small spool/winch system
- low speed
- limited force
- manual trigger (button or switch)

NO autonomy required for first test

---

## Control (v1)

Manual trigger:
- press button → assist engages
- release → tension drops

Optional v1.1:
- IMU detects forward motion
- auto trigger assist window

---

## Build Targets

### What must work

- anchor stability
- cable path consistency
- smooth engagement
- safe release
- measurable assist feeling

### What can wait

- dual leg
- AI adaptation
- perfect ergonomics
- production materials

---

## Test Plan

1. Stand with support
2. Trigger assist manually
3. Attempt leg lift / forward step
4. Compare assisted vs unassisted
5. Adjust cable angle and tension

Repeat until:
- motion improves
- system feels natural
- no instability introduced

---

## Success Criteria

- noticeable reduction in effort
- no violent or awkward motion
- anchors stay stable
- movement feels *better*, not different

---

## Failure Indicators

- belt twists or rides up
- thigh cuff rotates
- assist pulls torso instead of leg
- timing feels wrong
- motion becomes less stable

---

## Key Insight

Do not try to build the final system.

Build the smallest thing that proves the force path is correct.
